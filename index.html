HOW TO COMPARE CREDIT CARDS?

  1. Cashback Cards: These cards offer a percentage of your spending back as cash rebates, making them ideal for everyday expenses like groceries, dining, and transport. Look for cards that provide higher cashback rates in the categories where you spend the most. Be mindful of minimum spending requirements and cashback caps.
  2. Cash Rebate Cards: Similar to cashback cards, but rebates can accumulate over time, often without expiration. These cards are great for regular payments like utility bills or insurance.
  3. Airmiles Cards: If you travel frequently, an airmiles card allows you to earn miles on your future flights or hotel stays. Compare cards based on how quickly you can earn miles and additional perks like airport lounge access, travel insurance, or conversion rates for miles.
  4. Rewards Cards: These cards let you accumulate points that can be redeemed for a variety of goods, vouchers, or services. Some cards offer bonus points for specific categories like dining or online shopping. Make sure to check the expiration of points and the value of rewards.
  5. Annual Fees and Interest Rates: Consider the card's annual fee, which may be waived for the first year, and the interest rates if you plan to carry a balance.
